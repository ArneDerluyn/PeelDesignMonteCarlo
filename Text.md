# Peeldesign Business Case

## Introduction##

## Scope
This report falls in the framework of the BBBC project ‘Peeldesign’ where the aim is to enable circularity of interior products by temporary protection with a peelable coating. Lots of products are being replaced before the main constituent material has reached it’s end of life. Therefor the project team has researched the application of peelcoatings in three cases: exhibition stands, shopfitting and office furniture. Each of the cases has its unique requirements and usage periods.
Coupling these requirements, use cases and the researched materials we can look deeper in circular use cases and more specifically a circular business case. Main goal of this report is to highlight what drives the price of such a use case on product level and which conditions influence the validity of the business case.

This report is to be treated as confidential since it contains price information on specific products which are not to be publicly disclosed. 
## Cases
In the peeldesign project three use-cases have been defined with each different product use time. The first case is expo-stands where the use time of the scaffolding material is often only days, where the base material lasts 10 years or longer. 
Shopfittings can be renewed seasonally to highlight new arrivals, but on average a shop will update it’s interior design every three years. 
The third case is the application of peelable coatings to office furniture. These types of furniture are often supplied on a rental or lease basis and are renewed every five years. 

### Expo Stands
Currently BeMatrix offers their aluminium stand frames in two finishes, a powder coated frame and a brushed aluminium finish. The application of peelable coatings would allow standbuilders to match the colour schemes of their clients by applying the correct peelable coating. Today the only option is to powder coat frames in a specific colour, with the big drawback that these frames can only be used for projects with that specific client. Furthermore this increases the stock a stand builder needs to hold, and increases the investments they have to make.
The alternative currently on the market is the ECO+ frame. This is a brushed aluminium finish, which has the advantage of being universal and versatile for all projects and the drawback that in some designs the light metal colour contrasts with the graphical design of the stand.
The application of peelable coatings on ECO+ frames would allow the framebuilder to match the colour, whilst also protecting the frame against scratches. This service can fit in the current offering as a premium service in comparison to the ECO+ frames and to the investment of creating bespoke powder coated frames. 

### Shopfitting
The case of shopfittings is defined around a shelve unit in a shop. The wear and tear over years of use diminishes the visual appearance of the shelf which would negatively impact the overall look and feel of the shop.  
The application of peelable coatings can offer either a transparent protective coating on the original finish of the shelf, thus extending the valuable use time of the shelf. Or a peelable coating can offer a new finish, renewing it for a next use. A third option is that the shelf is temporarily coated with a highlighting colour to make certain products more attractive. This can be easily removed once the marketing campain has ended or has shifted focus. This adds value to the use of the shelf.

### Office furniture
Office furniture sees a lot of wear and abrasion on the surface which wears down the coating or finishing layer. But after a typical use the furniture itself still stands strong and could be re-used if the finish can be repaired. Peelable coatings can enable this repair by either being applied on top of the finish and serving as a protective layer, or applying the peelcoating as a new layer which can be easily removed for the next repair and use cycle. 
Method
Within the Peeldesign project WP1 Task 2 a report on the Eco-impact of the proposed cases gives an insight on how a re-use scenario of the peel-coated materials would look like. Based on this a more detailed analysis is presented in report “20251016 Protocols ADLR rev CSP” within WP4 task 1.

Both reports map a circular approach, using a peelable coating as enabler for these cases. Coupling this with the prices of the two types of peelable coatings which have been highlighted in earlier studies in the project being several types of HS Protect products and one product from Cardip enables us to compose a business case for these circular use cases. See report 20250519 - Prices Peelabel Coatings – ADLR for more details on the prices of these peelable coatings. 

To calculate the price of the circular use of these products with peelable coatings, each step of the circular protocol was quantified. This allows us to calculate a price per use or per lifetime and compare different use protocols with each other. 
In a second step a sensitivity study was performed to find the key parameters which drive the cost per use for each protocol. 
 
## Circulair Protocols
The circular protocols from report 20251016 – Protocols needed to be translated into cost calculations. 
Each block is a processing step in the circular use of the product, which also has an economic cost. These costs are calculated based on assumptions, reference values given by partners and the cost of materials reported earlier. Costs are calculated per timeframe (per use or per year) and the total cost over the lifetime, or the cost per use, can be compared between the different protocols. 

 

## Cost calculations
The costs are made up out of a summation of the following categories (not all are applicable to every case):
	Storage
	Transport
	Application peelcoating
	Application powder coating
	Removal of peelcoating (Decoating)
	Rental cost
	Purchase of base material
 
###Storage
Storage is estimated to cost 50 € per year per pallet stock. Price per use is given by 
Storage cost=(50 €)⁄12  *Months in storage*1⁄(units per pallet)
The number of months in storage is dependant on the protocol. 
###Transport
A transport within Belgium is estimated to cost 85 € per pallet.
The total transport cost for each product use is given by
Transport cost=85 €*number of transports*1⁄(units per pallet)
The number of transport changes in function of the protocol. 
###Application peelcoating
For the expo stands the price for the application of a peelcoating is estimated using the following assumptions:
4 frames at a time in the painting booth
15 minutes preparation and clean-up time
15 minutes spend on spray coating. 
Which gives a total working time of 30 minutes for 4 frames

The hourly rate of a worker is estimated to be 40 €/hr

The price of the peelable coatings is set on 2,3375 €/m² for HS protect and 14,62 €/m² for Cardip. A frame is equated to 1 m² since there is a lot of overspray.

This gives the following price per use
Application peelcoating cost=((hourly rate*time))/4+price peelable coating


For the cases on shopfitting and office furniture the following assumptions are made
20 minutes working time for the application of peelable coating on one product. 
Which leads to the following price estimate
Application peelcoating cost=hourly rate*time+price peelable coating

### Application Powder coating
In the case of expo-stands a comparison is made to the powdercoeated frames which are currently on offer by BeMatrix. Since the powder coating is applied by a subcontractor the price is assumed to be 9 € per frame for standard colours, and 14 € per frame for special formulated colours. 
removal of peelcoating
The cost of the removal of the peelcoating is based on the time needed to remove the coating and on the hourly rate of a worker. 
Based on a test in the laboratory, it takes about 7 minutes to remove the coating from a frame. The hourly rate is equal to the hourly rate used in the application of peelable coatings.

Purchase of base material
The purchase of the base material represents the initial cost for the purchase of the product. This can be used to differentiate between different types of coatings, where for example one coating might be more durable then another type, which could lead to earlier replacements of the base material. 
###Rental Cost
The rental cost is also specific for one of the use protocols in the expo-stand case. The rental cost is equal to 1/10th of the purchase price of a frame.
